# DoThis Organic Search Content Brief

## Strategy

DoThis is a newer domain, so the first content cluster targets specific program-building questions instead of relying on broad terms such as "workout" or "weight loss." Every article gives the complete answer before introducing the app. The conversion moment appears where static advice becomes difficult to personalize: schedule changes, equipment limits, progression records, sport workload, or recovery.

No keyword-volume estimates are included because no reliable paid keyword dataset was used. Priorities are based on live search-result intent, competitive page types, product relevance, and the content gap DoThis can credibly fill.

| Priority | Article / target keyword | Secondary intent | Observed competition | Content gap and DoThis conversion goal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Beginner gym workout plan | 3-day gym routine, beginner sets and reps | Large health publishers, gym brands, trainer sites | Many results list exercises but give weak progression and missed-day rules. Deliver a complete A/B plan, then offer schedule/equipment personalization. |
| 2 | Full-body dumbbell workout | home dumbbell plan, dumbbell-only routine | Health publishers and equipment brands | Provide two repeatable sessions, bench-free substitutions, and large dumbbell-jump guidance. Convert around owned equipment. |
| 3 | Upper/lower workout split | 4-day upper lower split, weekly schedule | Strength blogs and workout apps | Explain weekly placement and volume instead of only presenting tables. Convert around schedule and recovery management. |
| 4 | Push pull legs routine | beginner PPL, intermediate PPL | High-authority training publishers | Distinguish a sustainable three-day plan from a six-day plan and explain rotation after missed days. Convert around sequence memory. |
| 5 | Strength training for weight loss | preserve muscle while losing fat | Medical publishers and fitness brands | Separate lifting's muscle-retention job from calorie-burning circuits. Convert around combined workout, food, and recovery planning. |
| 6 | Body recomposition workout plan | recomp weekly schedule, strength and protein | Health and supplement publishers | Use multiple progress signals and a real four-day program without promising simultaneous rapid fat loss and muscle gain. Convert around integrated trends. |
| 7 | Progressive overload | when to add weight, reps, or sets | Training education sites and app blogs | Give an explicit decision hierarchy and explain when repeating a performance is correct. Convert around completed-set memory. |
| 8 | 30-minute workout plan | busy professional workout, short gym plan | Magazine and trainer content | Preserve progression and rest instead of forcing a frantic circuit. Convert around available-time adaptation. |
| 9 | Volleyball strength workout | volleyball conditioning, jump training | USA Volleyball, certification providers, strength sites | Count court workload, coordinate match placement, and include shoulder durability. Convert around sport-day scheduling. |
| 10 | Knee-friendly lower-body workout | knee-friendly leg exercises, alternatives | Medical publishers and trainer content | Avoid declaring universal "safe" exercises. Provide adjustable ranges, conservative alternatives, and clear clinical limits. Convert around preference organization, not rehabilitation claims. |

## Editorial Rules

- Exercise names must pass the DoThis catalog validator before pages build.
- Health, injury, nutrition, and training claims cite authoritative or primary sources.
- Do not claim clinical or professional review unless a named qualified reviewer completes it.
- Every article is usable without an app download or account.
- Keep one contextual CTA after the program and one at the end. No interstitials, countdowns, or forced signup.
- Review articles when the DoThis catalog, product behavior, or cited evidence materially changes.
